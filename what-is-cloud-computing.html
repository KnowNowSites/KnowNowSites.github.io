What Is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing means using computers and storage that belong to someone else over the internet instead of using your own computer for everything.

Simple explanation

Instead of installing programs or saving files on your laptop, cloud computing lets you access them online from large data centers.

These data centers are owned by companies like Google, Amazon, or Microsoft.

Everyday example

When you use Gmail, your emails are not stored on your computer. They are stored on Googleâ€™s computers and you access them through the internet.

That is cloud computing.

Why people use cloud computing

  • You can access your data from anywhere
  • You do not need powerful hardware
  • Updates and maintenance are handled by the provider

Is cloud computing the internet?

No. The internet is the connection. Cloud computing is what runs on top of that connection.

The cloud uses the internet to deliver services, storage, and computing power.

Summary

Cloud computing is a way of using remote computers over the internet to store data, run software, and perform tasks without relying on your own device.
